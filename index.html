Cell Phone Plans

MAY 2020

Prepaid cell phone plans are gaining traction. Every year, we see at least one company launching prepaid services. The major postpaid carriers are all launching prepaid flanker brands in order to gain market share. Some of these prepaid plans are gaining a lot of traction due to their competitive prices. Tech Dot compiled a list of the best prepaid cell phone plans.

1. Fizz Mobile

Fizz Mobile was launched in 2019 by its parent company Videotron. Videotron is a big regional player in Quebec. As they launched late in the prepaid market, they have very attractive offers to try to convince customers to transfer to Fizz Mobile.

Pros: Low cost, Customizable plans, 25$ Referral Bonus using this code 2A7ZU
Cons: Home address must be in Quebec or Eastern Ontario (Ottawa and surrounding area)

2. Public Mobile

Public Mobile is owned by Telus. Previously, their service was rated as pretty mediocre, but after being acquired by Telus, they improved a lot.

Pros: Frequent promos, Loyalty/Referral/Autopay discounts
Cons: 10$ Sim Cost, 3G speeds

3. Lucky Mobile

Lucky Mobile also launched late in the prepaid market. They were launched by Bell, which has one of the best networks in Canada.

Pros: Unlimited data for certain plans(throttled to 128kbps after going over limit), Cheaper sim card (4$ at Dollarama)
Cons: 3G speeds

4. Chatr

Chatr was launched by Rogers more than a decade ago. They were one of the first in the prepaid market.

Pros: Unlimited data for certain plans(throttled to 64kbps after going over limit)
Cons: 10$ Sim Cost, 3G speeds

5. Freedom Mobile

Freedom Mobile is owned by Shaw Communications. It was previously known as Wind Mobile. As a new company, their network had some connectivity issues, but they have come a long way.

Pros: Low cost (even cheaper when paid annually), Unlimited data for certain plans(throttled to 250kbps after going over limit)
Cons: Home address must be in their area of service, Might encounter connectivity issues
